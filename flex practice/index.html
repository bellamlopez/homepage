Queer and feminist social movements use digital technologies to build alternative information infastructures. These movements struggle to provide vital access to information using new digital tools, within conditions where that access is often precarious. Focusing on the transition from paper to digital based archival technologies from 1970s to present, Mcinney shows how media technologies animate the collective and unspectacular labor that sustains social movements, including antiracist and trans inclusive endeavors.
